How it works

Five steps. The parts that touch your data stay in your browser; only cleaned-up titles ever reach an AI.

  1. In your browser

    Your file stays local

    The bookmarklet's file is parsed on this page. It is never uploaded to our server.

  2. In your browser

    We clean it

    Channel names and handles are stripped or replaced with codes before anything leaves your device.

  3. AI, titles only

    An AI labels each item

    Only cleaned titles go out. The AI tags each one's topic and how emotionally charged it is, then the titles are deleted.

  4. Plain math, in browser

    Fixed rules decide your drift type

    Not the AI. Published thresholds you can audit — so the same data always gives the same answer.

  5. AI, then a second AI

    Your report is written and tone-checked

    No scare words, no "this caused that", no comparing you to others. If the tone check fails twice, you get a plain pre-written version.

Why split it this way? The AI is good at reading content but shouldn't judge you. The judgment comes from fixed, published rules — auditable by anyone.

Get your report

★

Easiest way — no scrolling at all recommended

Google will hand you your complete YouTube history as one file, with exact dates and times. Five clicks, no endless scrolling:

  1. Open takeout.google.com (sign in with the account you watch YouTube on) and click Deselect all.
  2. Scroll down the product list and tick YouTube and YouTube Music only.
  3. Click the "All YouTube data included" button and keep only history ticked. Then click "Multiple formats" and switch History from HTML to JSON. This matters — Drift reads the JSON one.
  4. Click Next step → Create export. In a few minutes Google emails you a download link (it's fast when you export only history).
  5. Download and open the zip. Inside Takeout → YouTube and YouTube Music → history you'll find watch-history.json — drop that file into the box below. Done.

Takeout doesn't include video lengths, so Engagement drift (shorter-and-more-often) can't be measured this way — Narrowing and Escalation work at full strength, on your real dates. The file is parsed in your browser and never uploaded, exactly like the bookmarklet's.

2

Open your history & grab it

  1. Open youtube.com/feed/history (or your Reddit profile history) and make sure you're signed in.
  2. Wait a second for the first videos to appear, then click the ◍ Grab my history bookmark you just made. A small bar appears at the top with a live counter and a Download my history button.
  3. Scroll down gently for a minute or two. The counter shows items and days collected — e.g. "120 items across 14 days". You don't need to reach the bottom: 15+ videos across 10+ days is enough for a report; a few weeks of depth makes it sharper.
  4. When the day count reaches a couple of weeks (or you've had enough scrolling), click Download my history. It saves one small file to your Downloads. Now go to Step 3.

Watch the days number, not just items — it's the day spread that makes the early-vs-late comparison honest. If scrolling gets tedious, stop and use the no-scroll Takeout option above instead: it covers everything without any scrolling.

Privacy, in one paragraph

The bookmarklet runs only on the page you click it on and saves its file straight to your device — it talks to no server. A Google Takeout file goes from Google straight to you; Drift never sees the download. Either way, that file never leaves your browser. Only cleaned-up video titles reach the AI service, which deletes them immediately and never trains on them. Your habit patterns — when and how long you watch — are computed entirely in your browser and are never sent. Before anything is sent, you see exactly what will be sent. All code, including the bookmarklet's full script above, is public.

  • Never blocks, filters, or recommends content
  • Never claims the algorithm caused your habits — patterns, not proof
  • Never sends your raw file, your name, or your habit data anywhere
  • Never reports to anyone unless you choose to share a summary
  • No scores, no streaks, no guilt
